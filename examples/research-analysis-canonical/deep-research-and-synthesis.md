# Deep Research and Synthesis

This page explains what happens after the graph has framed the analytical question and identified the route families.

## Step 1: run `DEEP_RESEARCH_AUDIT`

This node exists because current-reality questions are unsafe to answer from loose memory or generic intuition.

The research node should gather:
- authoritative sources
- current status and constraints
- realistic route comparisons
- known uncertainties

## Step 2: build an evidence map

The graph should not jump from raw sources to final answer.

It should first create an evidence map that organizes:
- what is confirmed
- what is uncertain
- what route families remain viable
- what constraints eliminate certain options

This keeps the later synthesis from becoming a pile of half-remembered facts.

## Step 3: synthesize only after grounding

Only once the evidence map exists should the graph produce the actual answer.

A strong synthesis should:
- explicitly answer the real framed question
- compare the meaningful route families
- state which routes are realistic, unrealistic, or speculative
- explain what evidence supports the answer

## Why this matters

A polished analytical answer is not trustworthy just because it sounds organized.

The graph creates trust by making the grounding process explicit before conclusion-writing begins.
