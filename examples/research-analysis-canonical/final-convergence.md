# Final Convergence

This page explains how the analytical branch closes.

## Final convergence depends on framing stability

A strong analytical branch does not only ask:
- do we have enough facts?

It also asks:
- are we still answering the right question?

That is why the graph includes an explicit decision after synthesis.

## Main closing nodes

### `QA_GATE`
Checks whether the synthesis:
- actually answers the resolved question
- respects the evidence map
- distinguishes realistic routes from speculative ones
- makes uncertainty explicit where needed

### `GRAPH_REFRESH` when needed

If the research reveals that the original framing was materially wrong, the graph should refresh.

Typical triggers:
- the user’s question really splits into multiple distinct decisions
- the dominant constraint is different than initially assumed
- the route-family clustering needs to be redone
- the answer would otherwise be a polished response to a nearby question

## What successful convergence looks like

A successful analytical convergence phase produces:
- a clearly framed answer
- route comparison grounded in current evidence
- explicit uncertainty where it truly exists
- no hidden drift away from the actual question

## What this example proves

This example shows that **AI Task Graph** is not only about quality-safe generation.

It is also a discipline for current-reality reasoning:
- frame first
- ground before synthesis
- refresh the graph if research changes the real problem
